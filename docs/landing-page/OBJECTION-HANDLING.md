## OBJECTION MAP (6 Core Objections)

---

### Objection 1: "It's just another CRM."

**Underlying fear:** He's been sold features that didn't change anything.

**Counter:**
> RainMachine is not a CRM. It's the system that makes your CRM actually work.
>
> Most CRMs give you a place to store leads and expect your agents to do the rest. RainMachine is the infrastructure layer on top — AI that contacts leads within 60 seconds, routing logic that sends the right lead to the right agent, dashboards that give you live visibility, and Claude AI reporting that tells you what's working every week.
>
> The difference: a CRM is a tool. RainMachine is a machine. One requires daily input. The other runs overnight.

---

### Objection 2: "I'll still have to build everything myself."

**Underlying fear:** He's bought software that required 40 hours of setup and still didn't work.

**Counter:**
> You won't. We configure it.
>
> When you sign up for RainMachine, we set up your routing rules, build your AI follow-up sequences, configure your GHL integration, and connect your ad accounts. You review the setup. You don't build it.
>
> On Growth and Scale plans, we also build and manage your Meta and Google campaigns — so the leads that flow into the machine are already qualified for your market.
>
> Your job on week one: answer our onboarding questions, review the setup, confirm your agent roster. That's it.

---

### Objection 3: "The AI won't actually book real appointments."

**Underlying fear:** He's seen AI demos that don't hold up in the real world.

**Counter:**
> Fair skepticism. Here's what we suggest: don't start with ads.
>
> Start with a **$1,500 Database Revival**. We run our AI against your existing dead lead database — people who filled out a form and never converted. No new ad spend. We contact them, qualify them, and book appointments directly on your agents' calendars.
>
> Most teams book 5–15 qualified appointments in the first 14 days from leads they'd already written off. That's the proof of concept — and it pays for itself before we ever talk about running a single ad.

### Objection 4: "I've been burned by vendors. What happens when this doesn't work?"

**Underlying fear:** He's paid for accountability before and got activity reports.

**Counter:**
> We have skin in the game.
>
> If your team doesn't have appointments on the calendar within 14 days of launch, your **next month is free**. Not a credit. Not a partial refund. Your next month free — because we missed.

### Objection 5: "What if it doesn't work for my specific team setup?"

**Underlying fear:** He has a complex routing situation, niche market, or non-standard agent structure that "solutions" always fail to account for.

**Counter:**
> Tell us before we build, not after.
>
> Every RainMachine setup starts with a strategy call where we map your exact team structure — how many agents, what market segments they work, how you currently route leads, what your GHL setup looks like. We configure the system to match your operation.
>
> If your setup is outside what RainMachine handles today, we'll tell you on that call — not three months and $15,000 later.

### Objection 6: "The price is too high."

**Underlying fear:** He can't mentally justify $4,997/mo without a clear ROI calculation.

**Counter:**
> Let's run the math together.
>
> You're probably spending $5,000–$15,000/month on lead generation right now. Between Zillow, Opcity, Meta, Google, and a team CRM subscription, $8,000–$12,000/month is common for a 10–20 agent team — with no accountability for what happens after the lead arrives.
>
> RainMachine Growth at $4,997/month includes the platform, AI follow-up, done-for-you Meta and Google campaigns, and dedicated support. One platform. One bill. One number to hold accountable.
>
> And the agent seat math: if you pass platform access through to your agents at $300/month, 10 agents generates $3,000/month. You're net $1,997/month for a complete acquisition machine. Many team leaders charge $400–$500/month per seat — at that rate, the system generates profit.

## FAQ — FULL SET

### About the Platform

**Q: Does RainMachine replace my GoHighLevel setup?**
> RainMachine integrates with GHL — it doesn't replace it. Your existing GHL sub-account becomes more powerful, not redundant. We configure the automation layer on top of what you already have.

**Q: How long does setup take?**
> Most teams are live within 5–7 business days of completing onboarding. Our setup team handles the technical configuration. Your main input is your agent roster, routing preferences, and existing lead sources.

**Q: What does the AI voice agent actually say?**
> It introduces itself as your team's AI assistant, confirms the lead's interest, answers qualifying questions about buyer/seller status and timeline, and books an appointment directly on your agent's calendar if the lead qualifies. It sounds human, it's conversational, and it's completely configured to your team's brand and market.

**Q: Can I see everything my agents are doing?**
> Yes. The RainMachine dashboard gives you a live feed of every lead, every agent's pipeline, contact attempts, appointments booked, and campaign performance. You see the whole operation on one screen.

---

### About Rainmaker Leads

**Q: Why do I need RainMachine to access Rainmaker Leads?**
> Rainmaker Leads are qualified, high-intent leads from campaigns we build and manage specifically for your market. These leads respond differently than cold list leads — because they were generated with the intent to be worked by an AI-powered follow-up system.
>
> Running Rainmaker Leads without RainMachine is like putting jet fuel in a Toyota. The platform is what makes the leads worth what they cost. We won't run fuel through a system we didn't build — it doesn't serve you, and it doesn't serve us.

**Q: What platforms do Rainmaker Leads come from?**
> Starter: Meta (Facebook/Instagram) only. Growth: Meta + Google. Scale: Meta + Google + YouTube + TikTok. All campaigns are built and managed by our team — you don't touch the ad account.

**Q: What does "market exclusivity" mean exactly?**
> One team per metro market per ad channel. If your market is open and you sign up for Rainmaker Leads, no other team in your market gets access on that channel. If your market is already taken, you can't unlock Rainmaker Leads for that area — even if you're on the platform.

---

### About the Guarantee

**Q: What exactly is the 14-day guarantee?**
> If your team doesn't have real, qualified appointments on the calendar within 14 days of your RainMachine launch, your **next month is free**. This applies to Growth and Scale plans that include Rainmaker Leads. The DBR offer has its own results timeline.

**Q: What counts as a "qualified appointment"?**
> A booked appointment with a lead who has confirmed interest, answered qualifying questions, and accepted a specific time on your agent's calendar. Not a lead who might call. An appointment that exists.
